Our Story

Born From Compassion.
Built for the Community.

Safe Housing Outreach Ministry CDO was founded with one urgent mission: to provide safe shelter and support to battered women and children fleeing dangerous and life-threatening situations. We have always believed that every person — regardless of their circumstances — deserves safety, dignity, and a roof over their head.


Over the years, our reach has expanded. While our commitment to survivors of domestic violence remains at our core, we now proudly serve the broader community across Detroit Metro, Michigan and Dallas/Kaufman County, Texas — offering emergency housing, transitional programs, affordable housing, homeownership pathways, workforce development, job readiness training, financial literacy, tax preparation, childcare, and wraparound stability programs.


We are a faith-rooted Community Development Organization driven by the belief that stable housing is the foundation of everything — health, education, employment, and family wellbeing. Established September 2016.

Our Programs & Services

From emergency shelter to homeownership readiness — Safe Housing Outreach Ministry CDO meets our neighbors where they are across Detroit Metro, Michigan and Dallas/Kaufman County, Texas, and walks with them toward lasting stability.

Emergency Housing

For individuals and families in immediate crisis across Detroit Metro and Dallas/Kaufman County, we provide emergency shelter placement, rapid-response referrals, and coordinated case management to ensure no one is left without a safe place tonight.

  • 24/7 emergency shelter placement
  • Transport to safety for families in danger
  • Recovery of vital documents: birth certificates, Social Security cards, state IDs
Get Help Now →

Transitional Housing

Our transitional housing program bridges the gap between emergency shelter and permanent housing in both Michigan and Texas. Separate units for women, teenagers, and families — 4 to 5 residents per unit.

  • Maximum stay: 182 days with 24-hour on-site supervision
  • 2–3 bedroom units with full living space
  • Required counseling sessions and life-skills workshops
  • Case management from intake through permanent housing placement
Apply Now →

Low-Income & Affordable Housing

We connect low-income families with affordable housing resources across Detroit Metro, Michigan and Dallas/Kaufman County, Texas — rental assistance, housing voucher navigation, and subsidized placement.

  • Rent based on income percentage using U.S. HUD guidelines
  • 100% of units reserved for low-income families
  • Government subsidies and housing vouchers accepted
  • All programs comply with the Fair Housing Act
Apply Now →

Home Ownership Programs

We guide families in Detroit Metro and Dallas/Kaufman County on the complete path from renting to owning their own home — with financial coaching, credit guidance, and down-payment grant connections. Homeownership is within reach.

  • One-on-one homeownership counseling
  • Down-payment and closing cost grant connections
  • Mortgage readiness and credit preparation
  • Buyer education workshops
Get Started →

Financial Empowerment

Our Pathways to Stability program is a holistic financial empowerment initiative helping low-income families in Detroit and Texas achieve long-term financial security and housing stability.

  • Free or low-cost tax preparation (sliding scale available)
  • Personalized financial planning and budgeting
  • Credit guidance and savings planning
  • Strategic use of tax refunds toward housing and stability goals
Learn More →

Workforce Development & Job Readiness

We prepare residents and community members in Detroit Metro and Dallas/Kaufman County for meaningful, sustainable employment — at no charge to residents within our transitional housing program.

  • Resume writing and interview coaching
  • Job search strategies and employer connections
  • Computer training and free lab access (open to the public)
  • Professional etiquette and workplace communication
  • Entrepreneurship track and career development counseling
Get Started →

Survivors & Family Support

Our founding mission lives on. We provide trauma-informed care, advocacy, and comprehensive support for survivors of domestic violence in Detroit Metro and Dallas/Kaufman County — helping women and children rebuild with safety and dignity.

  • Safe, confidential emergency placement — crisis line: 313-405-9606
  • Safety planning, DV advocacy, and individual counseling
  • Parenting skills, anger management, and life skills workshops
  • Spiritual guidance and mentoring — no additional fee
Get Help Now →

Homeless Prevention & Case Management

We walk alongside families every step of the way — from crisis stabilization through long-term housing stability. Our dedicated Case Managers provide personalized guidance, connecting each family to the resources, tools, and support they need to build a self-sufficient and stable life.

  • Emergency rental & utility assistance for families facing eviction within 14 days
  • Dedicated Case Manager assigned to each family from intake through stability
  • Individualized Housing Stability Plan — short and long-term goal setting
  • Budget counseling, credit building, and savings coaching toward homeownership
  • Coordination with landlords, agencies, and community resources
Get Help Now →

Community Outreach

Our outreach teams go directly into neighborhoods across Detroit Metro, Michigan and Dallas/Kaufman County, Texas — connecting residents to food, clothing, resources, and all of our programs.

  • Monthly food distribution: dry goods, canned food, and bread
  • Monthly clothing & shoe distributions — all ages, no charge
  • Vehicle donation program for employment transportation
  • Global outreach — South Africa: free medical clinics, job training schools, and water wells

Privacy Policy

Effective Date: January 1, 2024  |  Safe Housing Outreach Ministry CDO

Our Commitment to You
Safe Housing Outreach Ministry CDO is committed to protecting the privacy and confidentiality of all individuals who seek our services. We collect only the information necessary to provide housing, social, and community services — and we never sell your information to anyone.

1. Who We Are

Safe Housing Outreach Ministry Community Development Organization (SHOM CDO) is a faith-based 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization providing housing assistance, workforce development, youth programs, and wraparound community services to individuals and families in Detroit Metro, Michigan and Dallas/Kaufman County, Texas.

Contact: support@shoministry.org  |  (313) 405-9606  |  www.shoministry.org

2. Information We Collect

Online (this website): We collect only basic contact information voluntarily submitted through our intake request form — including name, phone number, email address, city, state, and a description of your service need. We do NOT collect Social Security Numbers, Driver's License numbers, financial account information, or upload sensitive documents online.

In person (official intake appointment): During your scheduled intake appointment, SHOM CDO staff may collect additional information required to provide services including government-issued ID, proof of income, Social Security information, and other documentation. This information is collected and stored securely in accordance with applicable federal and state privacy laws.

3. How We Use Your Information

Information collected is used solely to:

  • Contact you regarding your request for services
  • Determine eligibility for SHOM CDO programs
  • Connect you with appropriate housing and community resources
  • Comply with reporting requirements for nonprofit grant funding
  • Improve our programs and services

4. We Do Not Sell Your Information

SHOM CDO does not sell, trade, rent, or otherwise share your personal information with third parties for marketing or commercial purposes — ever. Period.

5. Information Sharing

We may share limited information only in the following circumstances:

  • With your written consent
  • With partner agencies to connect you with additional services (with your permission)
  • As required by law, court order, or to protect the safety of our clients or staff
  • With funders or government agencies as required for grant compliance (in aggregate/anonymized form only)

6. Data Security

We take reasonable measures to protect your information from unauthorized access, disclosure, or misuse including:

  • Secure encrypted email for all client communications
  • Password-protected systems for client records
  • Limited staff access to sensitive information on a need-to-know basis
  • SSL encryption on our website (https://)
  • Secure physical storage of paper records

7. Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Request access to the information we hold about you
  • Request correction of inaccurate information
  • Request deletion of your information (subject to legal retention requirements)
  • Withdraw consent for services at any time
  • File a complaint if you believe your privacy rights have been violated

To exercise any of these rights please contact us at intake@shoministry.org or call (313) 405-9606.

8. Children's Privacy

SHOM CDO serves families including children. Information about minors is collected only as necessary to provide services and is handled with the highest level of confidentiality in compliance with applicable child privacy laws.

9. Donations

Donation information is processed securely through PayPal. SHOM CDO does not store credit card or financial account information on our servers. Donor information is kept confidential and used only for processing your gift and sending tax receipts.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated effective date. Continued use of our website or services after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
